Foreword by the Authors

Rapid changes in global trade patterns are transforming the Canada-Brazil economic relationship. This edition of the Canada-Brazil Trade Report examines the evolving dynamics between these two major economies and what this means for future growth and cooperation.

Building on previous analysis, this report draws on comprehensive trade data to provide insights into provincial performance, product trends, and future opportunities. WTO-style simulations suggest that continued trade integration could boost bilateral commerce significantly over the coming years.

1. Introduction

The Canada-Brazil trade relationship has evolved significantly over the past five years. This report examines the complex and evolving dynamics between these two economies and explores how trade can continue to be a force for mutual benefit.

1.1 What is this report about?

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Canada-Brazil trade patterns, provincial performance, product trends, and future forecasts. It builds on previous analyses to offer insights into the opportunities and challenges facing bilateral trade.

1.2 Key questions addressed

  • How has total trade value and balance changed between 2024 and 2025?
  • Which provinces are driving growth and which are declining?
  • What products are leading trade expansion?
  • What does the 6-month forecast indicate for future trade?

1.3 Structure of this report

Section 2 examines trade overview and balance. Section 3 provides detailed provincial analysis. Section 4 analyzes product performance. Section 5 identifies growth drivers and contributions. Section 6 presents forecast analysis. Section 7 offers strategic recommendations.

7. Strategic Recommendations

Key findings

  • Total trade grew +16.0% from 2024 to 2025
  • Ontario contributed 68.8% of total growth
  • Cocoa and Cocoa Preparations led product growth at +212.2%
  • +2.2% growth expected over next 6 months

7.1 Focus on high-growth regions

  • Prioritize trade development in Ontario (68.8% of growth)
  • Allocate additional resources to trade missions in this region
  • Consider establishing dedicated trade representation

7.2 Capitalize on product momentum

  • Develop targeted marketing for Cocoa and Cocoa Preparations sector (+212.2%)
  • Organize buyer missions connecting Brazilian producers with Canadian importers
  • Explore value-added opportunities in this supply chain

7.3 Address underperforming areas

  • Investigate causes of Yukon decline (-100.0%)
  • Conduct stakeholder interviews to identify barriers
  • Consider targeted incentives to reverse trend

7.4 Prepare for projected growth

  • Begin supply chain planning for +2.2% expected increase
  • Monitor seasonal patterns to optimize inventory
  • Track forecast accuracy monthly

A.2 Analytical Methods

  • Growth Rate: ((2025 – 2024) / 2024) × 100
  • Market Share: (Provincial Value / Total) × 100
  • Contribution: (Provincial Change / Total Change) × 100
  • Forecast: Prophet time series with linear growth

  • All values in Canadian Dollars (CAD), nominal terms
  • Merchandise trade only (services excluded)
